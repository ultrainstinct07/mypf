## Overview

**[AD-Void](https://ad-void.void999.space/)** is a public offensive security knowledge base focused on Active Directory attack chains. It consolidates field notes, technique references, and scenario-driven write-ups into a single cheat sheet-style site — built to be useful during internal assessments, OSCP+/CRTA prep, and red team planning.

The site is organized around practical AD tradecraft rather than theory alone: how attacks chain together, what tooling to reach for, and how common enterprise misconfigurations become full domain compromise paths.

---

## What's Inside

### Attack Notes & Cheat Sheets

Structured reference material covering core AD offensive topics:

- **Kerberos abuse** — AS-REP roasting, Kerberoasting, delegation misconfigurations, and ticket manipulation
- **LDAP & ACL attacks** — DCSync prerequisites, shadow credentials, and dangerous permission chains
- **Lateral movement** — Pass-the-Hash, Pass-the-Ticket, WMI/WinRM pivoting, and relay scenarios
- **Privilege escalation** — local and domain escalation paths from standard user to Domain Admin
- **Persistence & evasion** — golden/silver tickets, SID history, and detection-aware tradecraft notes

### Scenario Walkthroughs

End-to-end attack scenarios that mirror real internal network assessments — from initial foothold through enumeration, privilege escalation, and domain dominance. Each scenario documents the decision points, tooling commands, and MITRE ATT&CK mappings an operator would use on engagement.

### Blog & Research Posts

Longer-form write-ups on AD-specific topics: novel misconfiguration patterns, tool comparisons, post-exploitation workflows, and lessons learned from professional red team and VAPT work (sanitized for public release).

## Why It Exists

Active Directory remains the dominant identity and access layer in enterprise environments — and the most common path to high-impact compromise in internal assessments. AD-Void exists as an open, continuously updated reference that bridges certification lab knowledge with real engagement tradecraft.

Whether you're preparing for OSCP+ AD modules, building an internal red team playbook, or reviewing defensive controls, the site is designed to be scannable under time pressure and deep enough for study sessions.
